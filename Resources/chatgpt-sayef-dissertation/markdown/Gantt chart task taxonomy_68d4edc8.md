The paper *A Literature-based Visualization Task Taxonomy for Gantt Charts* by Sayef Azad Sakin and Katherine Isaacs presents a systematic review of visualization tasks supported by Gantt charts across academic and commercial tools. The authors analyzed 35 research systems and 11 commercial platforms, mapping their features into a structured taxonomy of tasks. They categorized tasks into groups such as navigation, filtering, aggregation, and annotation, highlighting which were most and least represented. The work shows that while navigation and filtering are widely supported, higher-level tasks like correlation, prediction, and decision-support are less common. The taxonomy provides a foundation for evaluating existing systems and guiding the design of future Gantt chart visualizations. By bridging gaps between literature and practice, the study aims to inform scalable and user-centered solutions for analyzing complex temporal data. (≈250 words)
